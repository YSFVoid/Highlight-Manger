# Phase 4 Post-Launch Growth

This is the controlled growth plan for Highlight Manger after Season 2 launch.
Phase 4 is not a launch-blocker phase and not a rewrite phase. The ranked
foundation stays protected while real player, staff, and monitoring evidence
guides what gets improved next.

## 1. Post-Launch Priorities

Prioritize work from live evidence:

- Staff feedback from review, force-result, force-close, diagnostics, shop, and tournament operation.
- Player feedback from queue, room-info, voting, wallet, shop, and ranked clarity.
- Logs and diagnostics for unresolved matches, timeouts, cleanup failures, voice failures, reward anomalies, and interaction delay.
- Support burden, especially repeated questions or manual staff actions.

Capture and triage that evidence in
[`phase4-live-evidence-log.md`](phase4-live-evidence-log.md), then score the
first implementation candidate with
[`phase4-first-task-selection.md`](phase4-first-task-selection.md).

Protect trust:

- Do not destabilize rank, rewards, result privacy, staff permissions, or the official match flow for non-critical polish.
- Treat changes to rank, economy, anti-abuse, or staff override behavior as trust-sensitive.
- Use small measured improvements instead of broad rewrites.

Reduce recurring friction:

- Fix repeated confusion in queue, room info, host responsibility, voting, review handling, or shop fulfillment.
- Prefer improvements that remove staff work or make player actions clearer.

Expand secondary systems carefully:

- Tournament and larger shop/economy improvements are allowed after launch.
- Growth must stay proportional to staff capacity and real usage.

## 2. What Should Be Improved Immediately After Launch

Improve first:

- Live queue or match pain points confirmed by staff or player feedback.
- Ready-check, room-info, host responsibility, or result-voting copy that causes repeated confusion.
- Interaction delay on queue join, ready, room-info submit, vote submit, or staff force actions when logs prove it is visible.
- Anti-rematch hold thresholds only if live cases show the rule is too strict or too weak.
- Shop/catalog fulfillment if staff cannot support active items smoothly.
- Monitoring gaps that made launch incidents hard to understand.

Do not treat these as launch blockers unless they break live competitive operation.

## 3. Tournament Roadmap

First tournament pass:

- Better check-in handling.
- Clear checked-in and missing-player status.
- Staff-controlled manual check-in correction.
- Safer seeding tools.
- Clear tournament admin views.

Second tournament pass:

- Better bracket operation controls.
- Clearer tournament result visibility.
- Staff tools for no-shows, substitutions, and disputes.
- Better tournament reliability and recovery behavior.

Later:

- Richer tournament history.
- Additional formats.
- More automation when staff workflow is proven.

Tournament remains a secondary module. It should grow after the ranked core has
live stability.

## 4. Economy / Shop Expansion Roadmap

Immediate:

- Review live wallet and reward behavior.
- Confirm players understand how coins are earned.
- Confirm staff can fulfill every active shop item.
- Remove or hide any item that creates staff burden without clear value.

Build next:

- Expand the catalog only with supportable items.
- Improve order and ticket handling.
- Add clearer inventory or value displays if players ask for them.
- Improve shop help text when recurring confusion appears.

Defer:

- Advanced economy systems.
- Complex cosmetics.
- Marketplace-style features.
- Any reward loop that risks inflation or false expectations.

Economy growth should increase engagement without damaging trust.

## 5. UX / Admin Polish Roadmap

Post-launch polish should focus on clarity and operational speed:

- Improve queue and match card wording from real confusion.
- Make staff review and emergency actions easier to understand under pressure.
- Improve help text for ranked, wallet, shop, and staff review flows.
- Polish admin diagnostics only where staff needs faster decisions.
- Keep visual polish secondary to clarity, speed, and trust.

Do not rewrite launch-critical behavior for cosmetic reasons.

## 6. Monitoring / Diagnostics Improvement Roadmap

Track:

- Queue timeouts.
- Ready-check timeouts.
- Room-info timeouts.
- Unresolved matches.
- Anti-rematch holds.
- Cleanup failures.
- Voice reconnect or movement failures.
- Wallet and reward anomalies.
- Slow interaction paths.

Improve diagnostics when live operation shows a gap:

- Add clearer trend summaries if staff cannot tell whether an issue is isolated or systemic.
- Add incident-friendly health summaries when staff needs faster triage.
- Add anomaly summaries only when they help staff answer whether the competitive system is healthy right now.

Do not build a large dashboard until actual staff usage proves it is needed.

## 7. What Should Stay Frozen For Now

Protect:

- Core official match flow.
- Result privacy model.
- Locked rank fairness rules.
- Emergency-only staff power model.
- No casual point editing.
- No casual coin editing.
- Core queue, rank, and economy launch foundations.
- Anti-abuse direction unless live evidence proves adjustment is needed.

Reopen these only if live evidence shows they are hurting competitive trust or
staff operation.

## 8. Exact Phase 4 Execution Order

1. Collect first-week live evidence from staff notes, player complaints, diagnostics, logs, unresolved cases, and shop workload.
2. Fix confirmed post-launch pain in queue and match flow.
3. Tune rank or economy only when live data shows fairness or trust issues.
4. Improve staff workflow friction around review, force actions, diagnostics, rehost history, and audit visibility.
5. Expand monitoring where incidents were hard to understand.
6. Grow shop and economy in small supportable steps.
7. Start tournament check-in and seeding improvements.
8. Defer larger tournament, economy, and cosmetic systems until usage proves demand.

## 9. Risks To Avoid In Phase 4

- Chasing random features because launch is complete.
- Expanding tournaments before the ranked core has live stability.
- Growing shop or economy faster than staff can support.
- Breaking trusted rank, result, privacy, or reward behavior for polish.
- Adding staff powers that weaken competitive trust.
- Rebuilding systems that only need measured iteration.
- Treating guesses as evidence.

## 10. Strict Phase 4 Summary

Phase 4 is controlled growth after launch. Protect the ranked foundation, watch
real usage, fix real friction, and expand secondary systems carefully. Queue,
rank, economy, result privacy, and staff trust remain protected. Build what live
evidence proves is valuable, and keep Highlight Manger serious, fast, fair, and
professional.
